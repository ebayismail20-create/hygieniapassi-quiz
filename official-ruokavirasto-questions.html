üî¨ Microbiology & Food Contamination 8 questions
Q1RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Vacuum-packaged foodstuffs might contain microbes that cause food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
Some harmful microbes such as Listeria monocytogenes and Clostridium botulinum can reproduce without oxygen. Food may also have been contaminated before packaging. Vacuuming slows the reproduction of aerobic microbes but does not kill them.
Q2RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Temperature does not affect the reproduction of microbes.
‚ùå FALSE
Temperature is a significant factor affecting microbial reproduction. Microbes reproduce more effectively when the temperature is favorable for them.
Q3RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Microbes grow well in cooked rice at room temperature.
‚úÖ TRUE
Cooked rice has high water activity, which promotes microbial growth. Bacillus cereus in particular can form spores that survive cooking and multiply rapidly at room temperature.
Q4RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
A preservative destroys all microbes in a foodstuff.
‚ùå FALSE
Preservatives often work by binding water molecules, reducing water activity and hindering microbial growth, but they don't destroy all microbes.
Q5RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Some dairy products are made by using useful microbes.
‚úÖ TRUE
Beneficial microbes such as lactic acid bacteria, yeasts, and molds are used in the production of fermented dairy products (yoghurt, cheese), bread, and mold cheeses.
Q6RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
If raw milk is stored at refrigerator temperature, it cannot contain pathogenic bacteria.
‚ùå FALSE
Contamination during milking can introduce microbes, and some may reproduce even in cold storage. Pasteurization is needed to ensure milk safety.
Q7RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Bacteria that spoil foodstuffs can multiply rapidly if the temperature is between +6 and +60 degrees Celsius.
‚úÖ TRUE
This temperature range is the "danger zone" where many microbes thrive and reproduce quickly. Unnecessary storage or processing in this range should be avoided.
Q8RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
All microbial toxins are destroyed when food is heated to over +60 degrees Celsius.
‚ùå FALSE
Many microbial toxins are heat-resistant. For example, the toxin produced by Staphylococcus aureus is not destroyed by normal cooking temperatures. Prevention of toxin formation is therefore more important than relying on heating.
ü¶† Food Poisoning 7 questions
Q9RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Food poisoning always requires a large number of harmful bacteria.
‚ùå FALSE
Some food poisonings can be caused by very small amounts of bacteria or viruses. For example, norovirus and EHEC can cause illness with a minimal infectious dose.
Q10RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Deep-frozen berries might contain microbes that cause food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
Deep freezing only kills a few microbes and can keep viruses alive and infectious. Consumers are recommended to heat frozen berries at +90¬∞C for at least 5 minutes or cook them for 2 minutes.
Q11RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Food prepared by heating, but refrigerated too slowly, can cause food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
Bacteria like B. cereus can reproduce and produce spores if food cools too slowly. Foods should be cooled to +6¬∞C or lower within four hours after preparation.
Q12RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
It is not possible to get food poisoning from ice cubes.
‚ùå FALSE
Freezing does not kill all harmful microbes in food or water. If contaminated water was used to make ice cubes, the microbes survive.
Q13RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
A foodstuff may not be sold after its 'use by' date.
‚úÖ TRUE
The 'use by' date is for highly perishable products that pose health risks after this date. Such food cannot be sold or used. Note: the 'best before' date is different ‚Äî products can sometimes be sold after it.
Q14RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Steak tartare, i.e., a raw meat patty, can give you food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
EHEC bacteria can infect through eating food prepared from contaminated raw meat. Most microbes on meat surfaces are mixed into the meat during grinding, and they reproduce quickly in minced meat.
Q15RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Berries may collect viruses if the irrigation water is contaminated by, e.g., feces.
‚úÖ TRUE
Norovirus and Hepatitis A virus are important viruses that can infect via food and water. Berries irrigated with contaminated water can carry these viruses.
üç≥ Hygienic Work Methods 8 questions
Q16RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
The risk of food poisoning increases if an employee handles raw meat and vegetables using the same utensils or work surfaces when preparing food.
‚úÖ TRUE
This leads to cross-contamination, where harmful bacteria from raw foods are transferred to ready-to-eat foods via shared utensils and surfaces.
Q17RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
To avoid cross-contamination, food of animal origin (raw meat, raw poultry, raw fish) must be kept apart from food intended to be eaten without further heating (salads, cold cuts, smoked fish).
‚úÖ TRUE
Raw food and ready-to-eat food must not be handled with the same working tools, and proper hand hygiene must be observed between handling them.
Q18RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Salmonella can spread, for example, from utensils used for processing raw poultry meat.
‚úÖ TRUE
This type of contamination ‚Äî where microbes are transported from one food to another via direct contact, working tools, surfaces, or poor hand hygiene ‚Äî is called cross-contamination and is a common cause of food poisoning.
Q19RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Unpackaged smoked fish and fresh fish must not touch each other in the sales counter.
‚úÖ TRUE
These must be kept separate because smoked fish is often consumed without further heating, and any microbes from cross-contamination would not be killed by further processing.
Q20RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Packaging materials of foodstuffs may not be stored directly on the floor; instead, they must be stored on clean platforms.
‚úÖ TRUE
Storing packaging materials on clean, elevated surfaces prevents contamination from floor-level dirt, pests, and moisture.
Q21RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Pets are allowed in the kitchen of a food premises.
‚ùå FALSE
Pets are strictly prohibited in food preparation areas. Animals carry bacteria, parasites, and allergens that can contaminate food and surfaces.
Q22RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Deep frozen fish must be thawed in a refrigerated area.
‚úÖ TRUE
Frozen foods should be thawed in a way that minimizes the risk of pathogens multiplying. Thawing should occur in a refrigerator or other refrigerated space to prevent the surface temperature from rising too high.
Q23RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
If a foodstuff (such as liver casserole) has been taken out of a freezer to thaw, its shelf-life is longer than that of a similar foodstuff that has not been deep frozen.
‚ùå FALSE
Thawed food can spoil faster than unfrozen food. Freezing creates ice crystals that break down the food's structure. Thawed food's shelf life is shorter than fresh food.
üßº Personal Hygiene 5 questions
Q24RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
An employee must wash his/her hands if he/she has first handled raw foodstuffs such as raw fish, and then starts to handle cooked foodstuffs such as cooked broiler.
‚úÖ TRUE
This is crucial to prevent cross-contamination from raw to cooked foods. Hand washing is effective in reducing this risk.
Q25RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Good hand hygiene prevents food poisonings.
‚úÖ TRUE
Good hand hygiene is very important for preventing the spread of food poisoning microbes. Hands are the most common vector for contamination.
Q26RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Wounds on human skin can transfer food poisoning bacteria into foodstuffs.
‚úÖ TRUE
Wounds can contain Staphylococcus aureus and other bacteria. Food handlers with wounds must cover them with waterproof, brightly colored bandages and wear gloves.
Q27RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
An employee can spread contagious diseases, such as norovirus, through foodstuffs even without exhibiting symptoms.
‚úÖ TRUE
Asymptomatic carriers can spread norovirus and other diseases through food handling. After norovirus illness, an employee should stay away from work for at least two days after symptoms have ceased.
Q28RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
If an employee uses disposable gloves, the gloves must always be replaced after touching dirty surfaces or handling money.
‚úÖ TRUE
Disposable gloves protect food from dirt and microbes from hands. They should be changed after touching any potential source of contamination to prevent cross-contamination.
üßπ Cleaning & Sanitation 5 questions
Q29RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Cleaning the working surfaces immediately after work reduces hygienic risks.
‚úÖ TRUE
Immediate cleaning prevents the buildup of bacteria and pathogens, reducing cross-contamination risks.
Q30RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Washing and mechanical cleaning of the work surfaces can be replaced by using only a disinfectant.
‚ùå FALSE
Disinfectant cannot replace proper washing and mechanical cleaning. Physical cleaning must be done first to remove dirt and grease, then disinfection can follow as an additional step.
Q31RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Cleaning and sanitation equipment may be stored unprotected in the toilet next to the toilet bowl.
‚ùå FALSE
Toilet areas are prone to the spread of bacteria and other microbes. Cleaning equipment must be stored hygienically in a designated area, not next to toilet fixtures.
Q32RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Cleaning and sanitation equipment must be cleaned after use and maintained and stored hygienically.
‚úÖ TRUE
Dirty cleaning equipment can spread contamination rather than prevent it. All equipment must be properly cleaned, maintained, and stored after each use.
Q33RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
A rinse with water is sufficient for daily cleaning of a vegetable cutter.
‚ùå FALSE
A water rinse alone does not remove bacteria, biofilm, or food residue effectively. Proper cleaning with detergent and thorough drying is required.
üìã Own-Check (Omavalvonta / Self-Monitoring) 4 questions
Q34RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
All food business operators must carry out own-check (self-monitoring).
‚úÖ TRUE
All food businesses are legally required to implement own-check activities. This includes maintaining cleanliness, controlling temperatures, and ensuring the hygiene skills of staff.
Q35RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Monitoring the success of cooling and the temperature of stored foodstuffs is part of self-monitoring.
‚úÖ TRUE
Temperature monitoring and recording is a critical part of every food business's self-monitoring plan. Cooling times and storage temperatures must be regularly checked and documented.
Q36RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Raw materials and products that commonly cause allergies and contain gluten should be stored well-labeled and protected, e.g., in sealed packages.
‚úÖ TRUE
This prevents allergen contamination ‚Äî for example, gluten-free flour stored unprotected near regular flour. Proper labeling and sealed storage minimizes contamination risks.
Q37RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Microbes can end up in foodstuffs via dirt and dust.
‚úÖ TRUE
Dirt and dust carry microbes from the environment into food preparation areas. This is why maintaining clean premises and properly covering food is essential.
‚öñÔ∏è Food Legislation & Authorities 3 questions
Q38RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
An employee who handles unpackaged perishable foods on food premises must always possess a Hygiene Passport before commencing any work.
‚ùå FALSE
While a Hygiene Passport is mandatory, Finnish food legislation allows a grace period of three months. An employee must obtain the Hygiene Passport at the latest three months after starting work.
Q39RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
Microbes can multiply quickly in dirty tableware and cutlery.
‚úÖ TRUE
Food residue on dirty tableware provides nutrients and moisture for rapid microbial growth. Tableware and cutlery must be properly washed and stored hygienically.
Q40RUOKAVIRASTO ‚úì
If a shipment contains dairy products, meat products or fish products, it can be safely stored for a few hours at room temperature before it is transferred to a cold storage.
‚ùå FALSE
Perishable products must be transferred to cold storage immediately upon arrival. Even a few hours at room temperature allows dangerous bacterial growth in dairy, meat, and fish products.
