üî¨ Microbiology & Food Contamination 6 questions
Question 1 RUOKAVIRASTO
Vacuum-packaged foodstuffs might contain microbes that cause food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
Some harmful microbes can reproduce without oxygen, and food can be contaminated before packaging. Vacuuming primarily slows the reproduction of microbes that thrive in oxygen-rich conditions, but does not eliminate all risk.
Question 2 RUOKAVIRASTO
Temperature does not affect the reproduction of microbes.
‚ùå FALSE
Temperature is a significant factor influencing microbial reproduction. Microbes reproduce more effectively at favorable temperatures (typically +6¬∞C to +60¬∞C).
Question 3 RUOKAVIRASTO
Microbes grow well in cooked rice at room temperature.
‚úÖ TRUE
Cooked rice has high water activity, which promotes microbial growth. Bacillus cereus in particular can form spores that survive cooking and multiply rapidly at room temperature.
Question 4 RUOKAVIRASTO
A preservative destroys all microbes in a foodstuff.
‚ùå FALSE
Preservatives often work by binding water molecules, which reduces water activity and hinders microbial growth, but they don't necessarily destroy all microbes.
Question 5 RUOKAVIRASTO
Some dairy products are made by using useful microbes.
‚úÖ TRUE
Beneficial microbes such as lactic acid bacteria, yeasts, and molds are used in the production of fermented dairy products (yoghurt, cheese), bread, and mold cheeses.
Question 6 RUOKAVIRASTO
If raw milk is stored at refrigerator temperature, it cannot contain pathogenic bacteria.
‚ùå FALSE
Contamination during milking can introduce microbes, and some may reproduce even in cold storage. Pasteurization is needed to ensure milk safety ‚Äî refrigeration alone is not sufficient.
ü¶† Food Poisoning 6 questions
Question 7 RUOKAVIRASTO
Food poisoning always requires a large number of harmful bacteria.
‚ùå FALSE
Some food poisonings can be caused by very small amounts of bacteria. For example, certain toxin-producing bacteria or viruses like norovirus can cause illness with a minimal infectious dose.
Question 8 RUOKAVIRASTO
Deep-frozen berries might contain microbes that cause food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
Deep freezing only kills a few microbes and can keep viruses alive and infectious. Berries should be heated to at least +90¬∞C for 2 minutes before consumption if not eaten fresh.
Question 9 RUOKAVIRASTO
Food prepared by heating, but refrigerated too slowly, can cause food poisoning.
‚úÖ TRUE
Bacteria like B. cereus can reproduce and produce spores if food cools too slowly. Foods should be cooled to +6¬∞C or lower within four hours after preparation.
Question 10 RUOKAVIRASTO
It is not possible to get food poisoning from ice cubes.
‚ùå FALSE
Freezing does not kill all harmful microbes in food or water. If the water used to make ice cubes was contaminated, the microbes survive in frozen form.
Question 11 RUOKAVIRASTO
A foodstuff may not be sold after its 'use by' date.
‚úÖ TRUE
The 'use by' date is for highly perishable products that pose health risks after this date. Such food cannot be sold or used. Note: the 'best before' date is different ‚Äî products can sometimes be sold after it.
Question 12 RUOKAVIRASTO
If a shipment contains dairy products, meat products or fish products, it can be safely stored for a few hours at room temperature before it is transferred to a cold storage.
‚ùå FALSE
Perishable products must be transferred to cold storage immediately upon arrival. Even a few hours at room temperature allows dangerous bacterial growth in dairy, meat, and fish products.
üç≥ Hygienic Work Methods 7 questions
Question 13 RUOKAVIRASTO
The risk of food poisoning increases if an employee handles raw meat and vegetables using the same utensils or work surfaces when preparing food.
‚úÖ TRUE
This practice leads to cross-contamination, where harmful bacteria from raw foods are transferred to ready-to-eat foods via shared utensils and surfaces.
Question 14 RUOKAVIRASTO
An employee must wash his/her hands if he/she has first handled raw foodstuffs such as raw fish, and then starts to handle cooked foodstuffs such as cooked broiler.
‚úÖ TRUE
This is crucial to prevent cross-contamination from raw to cooked foods. Hands must always be washed between handling different food types.
Question 15 RUOKAVIRASTO
Packaging materials of foodstuffs may not be stored directly on the floor; instead, they must be stored on clean platforms, for example.
‚úÖ TRUE
Storing packaging materials on clean, elevated surfaces prevents contamination from floor-level dirt, pests, and moisture.
Question 16 RUOKAVIRASTO
Pets are allowed in the kitchen of a food premises.
‚ùå FALSE
Pets are strictly prohibited in food preparation areas. Animals carry bacteria, parasites, and allergens that contaminate food and food contact surfaces.
Question 17 RUOKAVIRASTO
Food handlers can taste food with their fingers during preparation.
‚ùå FALSE
Only clean utensils should be used for tasting (and each utensil used only once). Fingers introduce bacteria and contaminants into food.
Question 18 RUOKAVIRASTO
Easily perishable food products that require cold storage must be served at a temperature below +6¬∞C at all times.
‚ùå FALSE
During serving, easily perishable food products that require cold storage may be +12¬∞C at most. The maximum serving time under these conditions is 4 hours, after which the products must be discarded.
Question 19 RUOKAVIRASTO
Frozen food can be safely thawed at room temperature.
‚ùå FALSE
Thawing should occur in a refrigerator (+6¬∞C or below), under cold running water, or in a microwave. Room temperature allows bacteria to multiply on the outer layers while the center is still frozen.
üßº Personal Hygiene 6 questions
Question 20 RUOKAVIRASTO
Good hand hygiene prevents food poisonings.
‚úÖ TRUE
Good hand hygiene is one of the most important practices for preventing the spread of food poisoning microbes. Hands are the most common vector for contamination.
Question 21 RUOKAVIRASTO
Food handlers must always wear a hairnet or hat when handling food.
‚úÖ TRUE
Hair must be completely covered to prevent it ‚Äî and any microbes it carries ‚Äî from falling into food during preparation.
Question 22 RUOKAVIRASTO
Jewelry such as rings and watches can be worn while handling food.
‚ùå FALSE
Jewelry harbors bacteria and can physically fall into food. Rings, watches, bracelets, and piercings must be removed before food handling.
Question 23 RUOKAVIRASTO
A food handler with a wound on their hand can prepare food if they wear a regular bandage.
‚ùå FALSE
Only waterproof, brightly colored bandages are acceptable, and the wound must be completely covered. Regular bandages can fall off and contaminate food. Gloves should be worn over the bandage.
Question 24 RUOKAVIRASTO
You can handle food if you have a mild cold but no fever.
‚ùå FALSE
Any contagious illness ‚Äî including colds ‚Äî requires staying away from food handling. Even mild symptoms can spread viruses to food and other workers.
Question 25 RUOKAVIRASTO
Smoking is permitted in designated staff areas but requires hand washing before returning to food handling.
‚úÖ TRUE
Hands must always be washed after smoking, eating, drinking, or using the toilet ‚Äî before returning to food handling duties.
üßπ Cleaning & Sanitation 5 questions
Question 26 RUOKAVIRASTO
Cleaning the working surfaces immediately after work reduces hygienic risks.
‚úÖ TRUE
Immediate cleaning prevents the buildup of bacteria and pathogens, reducing cross-contamination risks. Surfaces should be cleaned and disinfected between tasks.
Question 27 RUOKAVIRASTO
Detergent alone is sufficient to disinfect surfaces.
‚ùå FALSE
Detergent cleans (removes visible dirt and grease) but does not disinfect. Disinfection requires approved sanitizers, heat treatment, or specific disinfectant products applied after cleaning.
Question 28 RUOKAVIRASTO
Hand disinfectant can replace hand washing when hands are visibly clean.
‚ùå FALSE
Hand washing with soap and water is always required. Hand disinfectant is an additional step after washing, not a replacement for it.
Question 29 RUOKAVIRASTO
Cleaning cloths should be changed daily or when visibly dirty.
‚úÖ TRUE
Cloths harbor bacteria and should be changed frequently. Using color-coded cloths for different areas (e.g., kitchen, toilet) helps prevent cross-contamination.
Question 30 RUOKAVIRASTO
Chemicals can be stored in the same area as food if they are on a separate shelf.
‚ùå FALSE
Chemicals must be stored completely separately from food products ‚Äî ideally in a locked, designated storage area to prevent any risk of contamination.
üìã Own-Check (Omavalvonta / Self-Monitoring) 5 questions
Question 31 RUOKAVIRASTO
Self-monitoring (HACCP-based own-check) systems are mandatory in all food businesses.
‚úÖ TRUE
All food businesses must have a documented self-monitoring plan based on HACCP principles. This is required by Finnish food legislation and EU regulations.
Question 32 RUOKAVIRASTO
Temperature monitoring of refrigeration equipment is part of the own-check plan.
‚úÖ TRUE
Regular temperature monitoring and recording of storage, cooking, and cooling temperatures is a critical part of every food business's self-monitoring plan.
Question 33 RUOKAVIRASTO
The FIFO principle (First In, First Out) should be followed in food storage.
‚úÖ TRUE
Older stock should be used before newer stock. This prevents food from expiring in storage and is a key part of proper stock rotation in the self-monitoring plan.
Question 34 RUOKAVIRASTO
Food can be stored in unlabeled containers if you recognize the contents.
‚ùå FALSE
All food containers must be clearly labeled with contents, date of preparation, and storage instructions. Personal recognition is not an acceptable substitute for proper labeling.
Question 35 RUOKAVIRASTO
If you suspect food is spoiled but are unsure, you should smell or taste it to check.
‚ùå FALSE
Never taste or smell potentially spoiled food ‚Äî when in doubt, throw it out. Many dangerous bacteria and their toxins are odorless and tasteless.

Question 36 RUOKAVIRASTO
A hygiene passport is legally required for anyone working with unpackaged perishable food.
‚úÖ TRUE
Finnish law (Food Act 297/2021) requires a hygiene passport for anyone who handles unpackaged, easily perishable foodstuffs in the food industry.
Question 37 RUOKAVIRASTO
The hygiene passport test consists of 40 true/false statements, and you need at least 34 correct answers to pass.
‚úÖ TRUE
The test contains 40 statements and requires a minimum of 34 correct answers (85%). The time limit is 45 minutes. The test is always written and supervised.
Question 38 RUOKAVIRASTO
Municipal food control authorities supervise the implementation of hygiene competence obligations in food businesses.
‚úÖ TRUE
Municipal authorities supervise hygiene competence in restaurants, catering kitchens, and food factories. The Finnish Food Authority (Ruokavirasto) guides and supervises the Hygiene Passport Examiners.
Question 39 RUOKAVIRASTO
A food business does not need to report to any authority before starting operations.
‚ùå FALSE
Food businesses must notify the local food control authority before starting operations. This notification is required under Finnish food legislation to ensure proper registration and oversight.
Question 40 RUOKAVIRASTO
Cleaning schedules and self-monitoring records should be documented and available for inspection.
‚úÖ TRUE
Documentation of cleaning schedules, temperature logs, and all self-monitoring activities is legally required. These records must be available for inspection by food control authorities at any time.
