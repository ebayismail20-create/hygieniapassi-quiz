‚ùå FALSE
Freezing stops bacterial growth but does NOT kill bacteria. When food thaws, bacteria become active again and can multiply.
Question 4

‚úÖ TRUE
Campylobacter is indeed the most common cause of foodborne illness in Finland, usually from undercooked poultry or cross-contamination.

‚ùå FALSE
Incubation periods vary. Some bacteria cause symptoms within hours (Staphylococcus), while others take days (Salmonella, Campylobacter).

‚úÖ TRUE
Bacillus cereus forms heat-resistant spores. Cooked rice left at room temperature is a common source of this food poisoning.

‚ùå FALSE
Only waterproof bandages are acceptable, and the wound must be completely covered. Regular bandages are not sufficient.
Question 8

‚úÖ TRUE
This dangerous toxin forms in vacuum-packed foods, canned goods, or garlic oil stored at room temperature.

Question 9

‚úÖ TRUE

‚ùå FALSE

‚úÖ TRUE

‚ùå FALSE

‚úÖ TRUE
Proper hand washing requires 10-15 seconds of thorough lathering and rinsing.

‚ùå FALSE
You must be symptom-free for 48 hours (2 days) before returning to food handling.
Question 15

‚ùå FALSE

‚úÖ TRUE
