1 FOOD POISONING & CONTAMINATION ‚Äî 8 Questions

QUESTION 1
Salmonella bacteria can multiply at room temperature.
‚úì TRUE Salmonella thrives between +6¬∞C and +60¬∞C (the "danger zone"). Room temperature (~20‚Äì25¬∞C) is ideal for rapid multiplication.
QUESTION 2
Campylobacter from poultry is the most common cause of bacterial food poisoning in Finland.
‚úì TRUE Campylobacter, primarily from undercooked poultry and cross-contamination, is the leading bacterial cause of foodborne illness in Finland.
QUESTION 3
Bacillus cereus food poisoning is commonly linked to improperly cooled rice dishes.
‚úì TRUE B. cereus spores survive cooking and germinate when rice is left to cool slowly at room temperature. Always cool cooked rice rapidly.
QUESTION 4
The toxin produced by Staphylococcus aureus is destroyed by normal cooking temperatures.
‚úó FALSE S. aureus toxin is heat-stable ‚Äî it survives boiling. While the bacteria are killed by cooking, their toxin remains active and can still cause illness.
QUESTION 5
You can always detect contaminated food by its appearance, smell, or taste.
‚úó FALSE Many dangerous pathogens (Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli) cause NO detectable changes in appearance, smell, or taste. Food can look perfectly fine yet be dangerous.
QUESTION 6
Frozen berries should be heated to at least +90¬∞C for 2 minutes before consumption to kill norovirus.
‚úì TRUE Norovirus can survive on frozen berries. Heating to +90¬∞C for at least 2 minutes is required to destroy the virus ‚Äî especially important in Finland.
QUESTION 7
A person carrying Salmonella can contaminate food even without showing any symptoms.
‚úì TRUE Asymptomatic carriers can shed pathogens without feeling ill. This is why hand hygiene is critical for all food handlers, not just those who feel sick.
QUESTION 8
Listeria monocytogenes cannot grow at refrigerator temperatures.
‚úó FALSE Listeria is uniquely dangerous because it can grow at temperatures as low as 0¬∞C. This makes it a serious risk in refrigerated ready-to-eat foods.

2 PERSONAL HYGIENE ‚Äî 8 Questions

QUESTION 9
Hands must be washed with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds before handling food.
‚úì TRUE Thorough handwashing with soap for 20+ seconds is the single most effective measure to prevent food contamination. Water alone is insufficient.
QUESTION 10
Food handlers are required to wear a head covering (cap or hair net) when preparing food.
‚úì TRUE Head coverings prevent hair and skin particles from falling into food. They are a standard requirement in professional food preparation.
QUESTION 11
An employee with diarrhea or vomiting may continue working if they wear disposable gloves.
‚úó FALSE Employees with gastrointestinal symptoms must be excluded from food handling immediately. Gloves do NOT provide adequate protection. They may return 48 hours after symptoms cease.
QUESTION 12
Wearing a wristwatch is acceptable when handling food, provided it is clean.
‚úó FALSE All jewelry ‚Äî watches, rings, bracelets ‚Äî must be removed before handling food. Bacteria accumulate under jewelry and cannot be adequately cleaned.
QUESTION 13
Cuts and wounds on a food handler's hands should be covered with a bright-colored, waterproof bandage.
‚úì TRUE Blue waterproof bandages are standard ‚Äî the bright color ensures they're easily found if they fall off. Disposable gloves should be worn over the bandage.
QUESTION 14
Nail polish and artificial nails are prohibited for food handlers, even when wearing gloves.
‚úì TRUE Nail polish can chip into food, and artificial nails harbor bacteria. Finnish guidelines prohibit both, as gloves can tear and don't eliminate the risk.
QUESTION 15
Disposable gloves are a replacement for hand washing ‚Äî if you wear gloves, you don't need to wash your hands.
‚úó FALSE Hands must be washed before putting on gloves and every time gloves are changed. Gloves are not sterile and can also harbor and transfer bacteria.
QUESTION 16
After a smoking break, a food handler must wash their hands before returning to food preparation.
‚úì TRUE Smoking contaminates hands with bacteria and chemicals. Handwashing after every break (smoking or otherwise) is mandatory before resuming food handling.

3 TEMPERATURE CONTROL ‚Äî 8 Questions

QUESTION 17
The temperature danger zone where bacteria multiply fastest is between +6¬∞C and +60¬∞C.
‚úì TRUE The danger zone (+6¬∞C to +60¬∞C) is the critical range. Food must spend as little time as possible in this range to prevent bacterial growth.
QUESTION 18
Cooked food not served immediately must be cooled from +60¬∞C to +6¬∞C within 4 hours.
‚úì TRUE Rapid cooling within 4 hours minimizes time in the danger zone. Use shallow containers, ice baths, or blast chillers to speed cooling.
QUESTION 19

‚úó FALSE This is a common myth. Waiting keeps food in the danger zone too long. Begin refrigerating promptly ‚Äî divide into smaller portions to cool faster.
QUESTION 20

‚úì TRUE Standard fridge temperature for perishable food is max +6¬∞C. Some items (fresh fish, minced meat) require 0 to +3¬∞C.
QUESTION 21
Hot food on a buffet or serving line must be maintained at a temperature of at least +60¬∞C.
‚úì TRUE Keeping hot food at ‚â•+60¬∞C prevents bacterial multiplication during service. Use bain-maries or hot plates and monitor temperature regularly.
QUESTION 22
Freezing food kills all harmful bacteria and parasites.
‚úó FALSE Freezing at ‚â§-18¬∞C stops bacterial growth but does NOT kill all bacteria. When food thaws, surviving bacteria resume multiplying.
QUESTION 23
The internal temperature of poultry must reach at least +75¬∞C to be safe to serve.
‚úì TRUE Poultry is a high-risk food. The core temperature must reach ‚â•+75¬∞C to kill Salmonella and Campylobacter. Always measure from the thickest part.
QUESTION 24
It is safe to thaw frozen food on the kitchen counter at room temperature.
‚úó FALSE Thawing at room temperature allows the outer layer to enter the danger zone while the interior is still frozen. Thaw in the refrigerator, under cold running water, or in a microwave.

4 STORAGE & TRANSPORT ‚Äî 6 Questions

QUESTION 25
Raw meat must always be stored below ready-to-eat foods in the refrigerator.
‚úì TRUE This prevents raw meat juices from dripping onto ready-to-eat foods. The fridge order from top: ready-to-eat ‚Üí dairy ‚Üí vegetables ‚Üí raw meat (bottom shelf).
QUESTION 26
The FIFO principle (First In, First Out) ensures that older stock is used before newer deliveries.
‚úì TRUE FIFO prevents waste and reduces food safety risks. New deliveries go to the back; older items stay in front to be used first. Check expiry dates regularly.
QUESTION 27

‚úó FALSE Chemicals must always be stored completely separately from food items, in a dedicated locked or clearly separated area, regardless of labeling.
QUESTION 28
Dry goods such as flour and sugar should be stored at least 15 cm off the floor.
‚úì TRUE Storing off the floor protects from moisture, pests, and contaminants, and allows proper cleaning underneath. Use shelving or pallets.
QUESTION 29
During transport, hot food may be allowed to cool naturally as long as it is covered.
‚úó FALSE Hot food must be maintained at ‚â•+60¬∞C during transport. Covering alone does not prevent cooling into the danger zone. Use insulated containers with heating elements.
QUESTION 30
All food products must be labeled with the name of the food, ingredients list, and allergen information.
‚úì TRUE EU regulations require clear labeling including 14 major allergens (highlighted in bold, italic, or CAPS). This applies to both packaged and unpackaged foods.

5 CLEANING & DISINFECTION ‚Äî 6 Questions

QUESTION 31
You can disinfect a surface without cleaning it first and still achieve effective sanitation.
‚úó FALSE Organic matter (food residue, grease) reduces disinfectant effectiveness. Surfaces must be cleaned with detergent first, then disinfected ‚Äî order matters.
QUESTION 32
Dirty, damp dishcloths are a significant source of bacterial contamination in the kitchen.
‚úì TRUE Wet cloths are ideal breeding grounds for bacteria. Change cloths frequently during the day and wash at ‚â•+60¬∞C. Disposable cloths are preferred in professional kitchens.
QUESTION 33
Using different colored cutting boards for raw meat and ready-to-eat food helps prevent cross-contamination.
‚úì TRUE Color-coding prevents mix-ups. Typical system: red = raw meat, green = vegetables, blue = fish, yellow = cooked food, white = dairy/bread.
QUESTION 34
Hand sanitizer (alcohol-based gel) can fully replace hand washing with soap and water.
‚úó FALSE Hand sanitizer reduces some bacteria but does not remove dirt, grease, or all pathogens (especially norovirus). Soap-and-water washing is always required first.
QUESTION 35
A written cleaning schedule (cleaning plan) is mandatory for all food establishments.
‚úì TRUE The cleaning plan must specify what, when, how, and by whom cleaning is done. It is part of the own-check plan and is checked during inspections.
QUESTION 36
Floors in food preparation areas should not be swept with a dry broom during food preparation.
‚úì TRUE Dry sweeping raises dust and bacteria into the air, contaminating food and surfaces. Wet mopping or vacuum cleaning is preferred, ideally done outside food preparation hours.

6 WORK METHODS & LEGISLATION ‚Äî 4 Questions

QUESTION 37
In Finland, the Hygiene Passport is legally required for anyone handling unpackaged perishable food for more than 3 months.
‚úì TRUE The Finnish Food Act requires a Hygiene Passport for all employees who handle unpackaged, easily perishable foods. The employer must ensure compliance within 3 months.
QUESTION 38

‚úó FALSE All food businesses, regardless of size, must have a written own-check plan based on HACCP principles. It covers hazards, monitoring, and corrective actions.
QUESTION 39

‚úì TRUE Use a separate clean spoon for every taste. "Double-dipping" introduces mouth bacteria into the food. Use a ladle to transfer food to a tasting spoon.
QUESTION 40
If you suspect a food product has been contaminated, you should discard it immediately and record the incident.
‚úì TRUE Suspect food must be removed from use immediately. Document what, when, why, and what corrective action was taken. When in doubt, throw it out.
